What Is Shiatsu Massage?

Pressure With Purpose

Shiatsu translates literally as "finger pressure" — but it is far more than that. Rooted in Japanese and Chinese medicine, shiatsu applies sustained, rhythmic pressure along the body's meridian lines to restore the flow of Ki, or life energy. Where energy stagnates, tension, pain, and illness follow. Where it flows freely, the body heals.

At J Massage SLC, our therapists are trained in classical shiatsu technique, reading the body's energetic landscape and responding with precision. Sessions are performed fully clothed on a comfortable mat — no oil, no draping, just pure therapeutic connection.

Why It Works

Five Ways Shiatsu Transforms the Body

Shiatsu addresses the body as a whole system — physical, energetic, and emotional — delivering benefits that reach far beyond ordinary massage.

Stress Relief

Shiatsu's slow, grounding pressure directly activates the parasympathetic nervous system — the body's rest-and-repair mode. The accumulated stress of weeks can dissolve in a single session as the body remembers what calm feels like.

Energy Flow Restoration

By clearing blockages along the body's meridian pathways, shiatsu restores the natural circulation of Ki — life energy. When energy flows freely, the body functions at its highest capacity. You leave feeling not just relaxed, but genuinely alive.

Headache Relief

Tension headaches and migraines frequently originate from blocked energy in the neck, shoulders, and cranial meridians. Targeted shiatsu pressure along these pathways provides rapid, lasting relief — often eliminating headaches that have persisted for days.

Anxiety Reduction

Shiatsu is among the most effective bodywork modalities for anxiety. The sustained pressure on specific acupressure points stimulates the vagus nerve, shifting the autonomic nervous system away from chronic stress activation and into a state of profound calm.

Digestive Support

Several key meridians governing digestion run through the abdomen and lower back. Shiatsu applied to these pathways can relieve bloating, reduce cramping, and improve overall gut motility — a benefit rarely associated with massage but consistently reported by shiatsu clients.

Common Questions

Everything You Want to Know

  • Shiatsu is a Japanese bodywork technique that uses rhythmic finger and palm pressure applied along the body's meridian lines — the energy pathways central to traditional Chinese and Japanese medicine. The word itself means "finger pressure." Unlike Western massage, shiatsu works with the concept of Ki (life energy), aiming to clear blockages and restore balanced flow throughout the body. The result is a deeply grounding experience that addresses both physical tension and energetic imbalance simultaneously.

  • Yes — shiatsu is performed fully clothed. No oil or lotion is used. We recommend wearing or bringing comfortable, loose-fitting clothing such as yoga pants, athletic wear, or soft cotton. The session typically takes place on a padded mat or table, allowing the therapist full leverage to apply precise pressure along the meridian lines. This makes shiatsu an excellent option for clients who prefer not to undress or who are new to bodywork.

  • Swedish massage uses oil-based strokes on bare skin to promote muscular relaxation through Western anatomical principles. Shiatsu works through clothing using sustained pressure on specific acupressure points and meridian pathways rooted in Japanese and Chinese medicine. Swedish tends to feel flowing and rhythmic; shiatsu feels grounding and intentional — more like sustained pressure holds than strokes. Swedish is ideal for full-body muscular relaxation; shiatsu excels at addressing energetic imbalances, headaches, anxiety, and chronic fatigue.

  • Shiatsu is particularly well-suited for anxiety relief. The slow, sustained pressure applied along the nervous system's pathways activates the parasympathetic response — the body's rest-and-digest mode — which directly counteracts the fight-or-flight state that underlies anxiety. Many clients report feeling profoundly calm and emotionally settled after a session, with benefits that extend well beyond the massage table. Regular shiatsu has been shown to reduce cortisol levels, improve sleep quality, and create lasting improvement in anxiety symptoms.
