Six Benefits of Foot Massage & Head Spa

From the soles of your feet to the crown of your head — these treatments reach where standard massage cannot.

Scalp & Hair Health

A head spa increases blood circulation to hair follicles by up to 40%, delivering oxygen and nutrients that promote hair growth and thickness. The deep conditioning component hydrates the scalp, reduces dandruff, and restores natural oil balance that harsh shampoos strip away over time.

Better Sleep Quality

The solar plexus reflex point — located in the center of the foot's arch — is one of the most powerful points for inducing deep sleep. Combined with the head spa's ability to dissolve occipital tension and slow racing thoughts, these treatments consistently produce some of the most restorative sleep clients experience.

Foot reflexology stimulates circulatory reflex zones that improve blood and lymphatic flow throughout the body. The head spa stimulates the extensive network of blood vessels in the scalp. Together, these treatments improve oxygen delivery, accelerate cellular repair, and help the body clear accumulated metabolic waste more efficiently.

Headache & Migraine Relief

Both treatments are remarkably effective for headache relief through different mechanisms. Head spa acupressure directly releases tension in the muscles that cause tension headaches. Foot reflexology works the head and sinus reflex zones on the toes, reducing the inflammation and vascular pressure that drive migraines.

Organ Function & Detox

Reflexology's greatest differentiator from regular massage is its ability to influence internal organ function. Working the kidney zones improves filtration. The liver zone stimulates detoxification. The digestive reflex zones soften cramping and improve motility. This is systemic therapeutic work that reaches organs no massage table can touch.

Everything You Want to Know

  • J Massage SLC at 677 S 200 W does not currently offer foot massage or head spa services at this location. However, our sister location — Elite Spa Utah at 1136 S State St in downtown Salt Lake City — specializes in exactly these treatments. Both locations share the same commitment to five-star service and therapeutic excellence. You can book directly at elitespautah.com.

  • Foot reflexology is a therapeutic practice based on the principle that specific points on the sole of the foot correspond to organs, glands, and systems throughout the body. A trained reflexologist applies intentional, targeted pressure to these zones to promote healing in distant body parts. It differs from a standard foot massage, which focuses only on the muscles and soft tissue of the foot itself. Reflexology is systemic — it reaches the liver, kidneys, digestive tract, lungs, and nervous system through the feet. Elite Spa Utah's reflexologists are trained specifically in therapeutic-grade foot reflexology.

  • A head spa is a premium scalp and hair treatment that combines deep scalp massage, hot oil conditioning, and targeted acupressure. The treatment typically begins with a scalp analysis, followed by a clarifying scalp treatment to remove buildup, then a deep scalp massage to stimulate circulation and release tension held in the head, neck, and shoulders. A nourishing oil or serum is worked through the hair, followed by a scalp rinse. The result is profound relaxation alongside real, measurable improvement in scalp health and hair condition. Elite Spa Utah has a dedicated Head Spa Studio designed specifically for this experience.

  • A regular foot massage focuses on relieving tension in the muscles, tendons, and soft tissue of the foot and lower leg. It feels great and has real therapeutic value for the foot itself. Foot reflexology uses a different approach — applying specific, mapped pressure to reflex zones that correspond to organs and systems throughout the body. The pressure techniques are distinct, the intent is different (systemic vs. local), and the effects are felt throughout the body, not just in the foot. Many clients feel sensations in their abdomen, chest, or head during a reflexology session, which rarely happens during a standard foot massage.
