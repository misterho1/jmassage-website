What Is Infrared Sauna?

Deep Heat. Real Results.

Traditional saunas heat the air; infrared saunas heat you. Far-infrared wavelengths are absorbed directly by the body's tissue, elevating core temperature from within at a comfortable ambient temperature of 120–150°F. The deeper penetration triggers a more productive sweat, one that carries a higher concentration of toxins, heavy metals, and metabolic waste compared to conventional heat.

At J Massage SLC, the infrared sauna is designed to function as both a standalone wellness ritual and a precision upgrade to any massage service. Pre-massage it softens the tissue; post-massage it extends the recovery response. Either way, your body leaves in a better state than it arrived.

Why It Works

Six Benefits of Infrared Heat

Far-infrared therapy delivers measurable physiological results, not just warmth. These benefits are supported by a growing body of peer-reviewed research.

Detoxification

Infrared-induced sweat contains a measurably higher concentration of heavy metals, BPA, phthalates, and other environmental toxins than sweat produced by exercise alone. Consistent sessions support the body's natural elimination pathways in a way no topical detox product can match.

Muscle Recovery

Infrared heat increases blood flow to fatigued muscle tissue, accelerating the removal of lactic acid and the delivery of oxygen and nutrients needed for repair. Athletes using infrared sauna post-training consistently report reduced soreness and faster recovery between sessions.

Improved Circulation

As core temperature rises, the cardiovascular system responds by dilating blood vessels and increasing cardiac output, producing circulatory benefits similar to moderate aerobic exercise. Improved microcirculation supports tissue health throughout the body, including peripheral areas that typically receive poor blood flow.

Skin Health

Deep sweating flushes the pores of impurities, bacteria, and dead skin cells that accumulate between showers. The increased circulation delivers nutrients to the skin's surface layers, supporting collagen production and improving tone, elasticity, and overall radiance with consistent use.

Stress Reduction

The warmth of infrared sauna activates the parasympathetic nervous system, lowering cortisol and triggering the same relaxation response as meditation or yoga. The quiet, private environment amplifies this effect. Regular sauna users report improved sleep quality, reduced anxiety, and a lasting sense of calm that extends well beyond the session itself.

Weight Support

The cardiovascular demand of maintaining elevated core temperature burns calories at a rate comparable to light exercise, research estimates 300–600 calories per 30-minute session depending on the individual. While not a replacement for exercise, infrared sauna is a meaningful complement to a healthy lifestyle, particularly for those with limited mobility or in recovery from injury.

Common Questions

Everything You Want to Know

  • A traditional sauna heats the air around you to very high temperatures, typically 180–200°F, which in turn heats your skin from the outside in. An infrared sauna uses far-infrared light wavelengths to directly penetrate body tissue up to 1.5 inches deep, raising your core temperature from the inside out. Because the mechanism is direct rather than convective, infrared saunas operate at much lower air temperatures (120–150°F) while producing a deeper, more comfortable sweat. Most people find infrared sessions significantly more tolerable, especially those who find traditional saunas oppressively hot or claustrophobic. The deeper tissue penetration also means more effective detoxification and muscle recovery per minute of exposure.

  • Our standard infrared sauna sessions are 30 minutes, which is the clinically optimal duration for most benefits, long enough to reach meaningful core temperature elevation and produce a productive sweat, short enough to avoid dehydration or fatigue. First-time users sometimes prefer 20 minutes while their body acclimates. We recommend arriving hydrated and drinking water before, during, and after your session. Sessions can be booked as a standalone treatment or as an add-on before or after any massage service.

  • Yes, and it is one of the most effective wellness combinations available. Using the infrared sauna before your massage pre-warms the muscles and soft tissue, allowing your therapist to work at greater depth with less pressure and less post-session soreness. Using it after massage prolongs the muscle relaxation response, supports lymphatic drainage of metabolic waste released during the session, and extends the parasympathetic state. The combination pairs especially well with deep tissue and sports massage, where muscle preparation and recovery are primary goals. Simply mention your preference when booking and we will schedule both seamlessly.

  • For general wellness and stress reduction, two to three sessions per week is a common recommendation and the range supported by most research. For acute muscle recovery, after intense training, a physically demanding week, or an injury, daily sessions for four to seven days can accelerate healing significantly. For detoxification goals, a consistent schedule of three to four sessions per week for four to six weeks produces the most notable results. There is no clinically established maximum for healthy adults, though staying well hydrated and listening to your body is always the primary guideline. Our team can help you build a schedule that fits your goals and timeline.
